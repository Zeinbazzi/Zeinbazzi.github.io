About Me

I am a PhD candidate at the Argelander Institute for Astronomy at the University of Bonn, where I focus on the study of molecular clouds and galactic environments. My research combines observational astronomy, data science, and computational methods to explore the mechanisms driving galaxy evolution and star formation. I'm deeply interested in how scientific discovery can be communicated across disciplines and cultures.

Research

My research investigates the life cycle of molecular clouds in a range of galactic contexts, aiming to understand their structure, formation, and influence on star formation. I analyze data from leading space observatories (e.g., ALMA, JWST) and simulate physical processes using computational models. Additional interests include AGN feedback, cosmic dust properties, and the impact of environment on interstellar medium dynamics.

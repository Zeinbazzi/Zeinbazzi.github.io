About Me

I am a PhD candidate at the Argelander Institute for Astronomy at the University of Bonn, where I focus on the study of molecular clouds and galactic environments. My research combines observational astronomy, data science, and computational methods to explore the mechanisms driving galaxy evolution and star formation. I'm deeply interested in how scientific discovery can be communicated across disciplines and cultures.

Research

Project: The EDGE-CALIFA Survey ‚Äì Investigating the impact of active galactic nucleus (AGN) feedback on molecular gas and star formation across different stages of galaxy evolution.

Publication: A&A, 697, A149 (2025)

Main Findings

  • AGN host galaxies generally have higher molecular gas masses (Mmol) than non-active counterparts, especially in the star-forming and mixed (MX) stages.
  • Despite higher Mmol, AGN hosts exhibit lower star formation efficiency (SFE) and specific star formation rate (sSFR), particularly in the SF and MX classes.
  • AGN feedback does not show strong instantaneous effects on global galaxy properties ‚Äî trends in SFE and fmol are largely consistent with non-AGN galaxies.
  • The QnR (quiescent nuclear ring) stage shows slightly reduced Mmol and fmol in AGN hosts, possibly due to gas inflow fueling black hole growth.
  • Results support the view that long-term cumulative AGN activity, rather than instantaneous feedback, may drive quenching.
